2021

Web Design Trends

Today

Nothing online stays consistent for long and web design trends are no exception. Looking at today's trends can provide us with a glimpse of where the web is headed. See some notable trends below.

01

Parallax

Parallax is the optical illusion that happens when objects near the viewer appear to move faster than objects farther away. This depth is created through the foreground and background creates an immersive experience for the viewer.

The Story of Jess & Russ

In this beautiful example of Parallax Scrolling, Jess and Russ tell the story of how they met.

Toyfight

In this immersive example, Toyfight brings both Parallax and 3D art into their experience.

Firewatch

In this example they provide a subtle but striking hero example of Parallax Scrolling.

02

Neumorphism

Neumorphism has been gaining a lot of traction with it's angle of minimalist realism. This style is a successor of skeuomorphism - a design approach that incorporates renderings of familiar, outdated materials into current designs. This trend replaced flat design. This trend resembles digital embossing or debossing. It heightens the user's connection to the design they are interacting with.

03

Comfy Colors

Due to the digital nature of the job market these days - most people spend a majority of their time on computers. Due to this, many experience eye strain. Web designers have been taking into account color schemes that are easier on the eyes. This explains the popularity of the dark mode trend. Many designers are finding middle ground in soft color palettes that are less jarring.

ZenDesk

ZenDesk creates the feeling of a calm online environment with their cool palette.

Pico

With a very minimalistic approach, Pico chooses and earthy palette creating a comfy, soft impression.

Wolf & Son

Wolf & Son uses a muted palette throughout their site while maintaining a complimentary look.

04

Animations

A good animation will capture a visitor's interest, without distracting them from the main information you want them to see on the page. It add more engagement and more personality to your site. There are some different ways to interactively add animations to your website. Below are some examples.

05

Unique Fonts

Fonts can add style, direction, balance, heirarchy, and draw attention to areas of your site you want visitors to go to. Above all, when picking a font you will want to make sure it is easy to read.

Emergence

Using serif fonts, outlines, and other typography techniques - Emergence isn't afraid to speak using type.

Ginventory

With a focus on font, Ginventory captures the audience with interactive scrolling and beautiful touches.

Academia Olivia

Custom lettering is in and there's a market for it. Academia Olivia has a beautifully balanced design features script.

06

Minimalism

Having plenty of whitespace continues to be a dominating trend because it makes your website more usable. You aren't overstimulating visitors with too much information. It helps guide site visitors to more important areas of your site. Minimalism will continue to be a dominating trend well into the future. It makes things easy - which is what users want.

07

Integrated Video

Video's popularity cannot be denied. When used well, video is an incredibly engaging medium. No, not popups - but creating engaging videos that act as a natural part of your web design. They inform customers about products, aren't long, and improve SEO.

08

Custom Illustrations

Web Design has evolved with designers now creating work that connects with people on a more personal level. 3D and custom illustrations now communicate and inform the consumer, often while creating an immersive and interactive experience.

Ya Ya

Sennep

Sweet Magnolia Gelato Co.

09

Horizontal Scrolling

What once used to be a faux-pas, horizontal scroll is having a comeback. More web designers are experimenting with horizontal scroll. Those who do it best break the pattern not for the sake of being different, but as a practical way to disclose secondary information progressively, like an image gallery. The biggest thing to keep in mind is to allow alternate ways to navigate, and know what content is appropriate to display using this feature.

10

Personalization

Web designers try their best to identify and understand who their target audience is when building a site. Your target audience will always be made up of individuals who have different preferences and priorities, so how do you cater to all of them? A growing trend in web design is to create websites that serve up a questionnaire or other interactive feature that lets the visitor tell you what they want in order to deliver them the content or products that best match their interests.

Amazon

With an unlimited database of products, Amazon is constantly analyzing your previous buying behavior and promoting products they think you might like.

Stitch Fix

Stitch Fix uses the information you provide about your size, shape, and personal style, compare it with others, and then a stylist selects outfits.

Spotify

Spotify has a massive collection of music you can choose from and listen to. They take music you've listened to and make recommendations depending on your choices.
